Warranty — how Kora supports OEM policies

Kora does not manufacture goods; warranty is driven by the OEM or supplier policy. We act as your facilitator for claims and communication.

1. What Warranty Means at Kora

Warranty for products sourced through Kora is primarily determined by the OEM or supplier in the country of origin. We help you initiate and follow up on claims, but final decisions rest with the supplier.

2. Durations & Coverage

Warranty coverage can differ significantly by category:

  • Consumer electronics may carry 3–12 month limited warranties.
  • Specialized industrial or automotive components may have limited or no warranty.
  • Consumables and wear‑and‑tear items are usually excluded from warranty.

3. Eligibility

To qualify for warranty support:

  • The defect must be a manufacturing defect, not caused by misuse, incorrect installation or modification.
  • The claim must fall within the time window stated (if any) on the OEM/supplier documentation.
  • The item must be accompanied by proof of purchase and any serial/lot details required.

4. Claim Process

  1. Contact Kora support with your order number, description and evidence (photos/videos).
  2. We review and forward the claim to the OEM/supplier with any required technical details.
  3. The supplier evaluates and issues a decision: repair, replacement, partial credit or rejection.

International warranty processing can take 15–45 days depending on inspections, shipping back to origin and supplier workload.

5. Limitations

  • Supplier/OEM decisions are final; Kora cannot override them.
  • Re‑export duties, diagnostic fees and shipping costs may apply unless explicitly covered by the supplier.
  • Unauthorised repairs or modifications usually void warranty.
